About Me

I am a recent graduate from John Jay College of Criminal Justice who has many passions, including playing guitar, working out, painting, and writing. But one area, in particular, that exceeds the rest is my passion for programming. It is a passion that originated back when I was in High School, where I discovered the countless ways technology has impacted and shaped our way of living. This newfound realization motivated me to learn programming. During those years, I learned to code in Python, Java, and Ruby and partook in numerous hackathons and coding programs.


During my attendance at John Jay, I focus my studies on Computer Science and Mathematics, allowing me to greatly enhance my comprehension of numerous topics crucial to software development. Topics such as Object-Oriented Programming, Computer Architecture, Cryptography, Cryptoanalysis, and Computer Algorithms, to name a few.


Outside of instituitions, I also spend a lot of time programming independently and in teams, in addition to learning concepts that weren't covered in school or vaguely discussed. My desire to become a proficient software developer led me to learn various programming frameworks, languages, and algorithms.
